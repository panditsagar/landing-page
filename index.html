Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

I have answered all common questions below that you might have about the workshop. For any further queries, please contact:

Do I need a diagnosis of Health Anxiety to join the workshop?

No, a formal diagnosis is not required. This workshop is designed for anyone experiencing symptoms of health anxiety, regardless of whether they have a medical diagnosis. Our focus is on providing tools and techniques to manage anxiety symptoms and improve your mental well-being.

I tried therapy and it didn't help. What makes this workshop different?

This workshop uses a unique blend of NLP, MBCT, Cognitive Neuroscience, and Eastern Healing Modalities, developed by Lalit Taneja. Unlike traditional therapy which can sometimes be passive, our workshop is highly interactive, focusing on real-time implementation of strategies and personalized guidance to ensure you gain practical, life-changing skills.

Will I get medical advice or diagnoses in the workshop?

No, this workshop does not provide medical advice or diagnoses. It is an educational and coaching program aimed at helping individuals manage their anxiety symptoms through specific techniques and tools. We encourage participants to consult with healthcare professionals for any medical concerns or diagnoses.

I'm shy about talking in groups. Will the workshop still be useful for me?

Absolutely! While there are interactive elements, participation in group discussions is entirely optional. You can engage as much or as little as you feel comfortable. The core techniques and learning can be absorbed effectively even if you prefer to observe and practice privately.

Will focusing on health anxiety in the workshop make me more anxious about my health?

Our approach is designed to help you confront and understand your anxiety in a safe and structured environment, providing you with tools to reduce anxiety rather than increase it. We focus on empowering you with coping mechanisms and strategies for calm, not dwelling on fear.

Could this workshop lead me to wrongly diagnose myself or misread my symptoms?

The workshop explicitly avoids providing medical diagnoses. Its purpose is to equip you with self-management techniques for anxiety. We always recommend consulting with a qualified medical professional for any health concerns or symptoms you may have. The content is for educational and self-help purposes only.
